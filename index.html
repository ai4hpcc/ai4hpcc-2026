Description

The AI4HPCC workshop explores the rapidly growing intersection between artificial intelligence (AI) and high-performance compiler construction, focusing on how large language models (LLMs), reinforcement learning, and other AI-driven methods can revolutionize the way we design, optimize, and deploy compilers for modern and emerging computing architectures, in both academic research and industrial practice.

As high-performance computing systems evolve to include heterogeneous processors, accelerators, advanced memory hierarchies, and novel interconnects, compiler design faces unprecedented complexity. Traditional heuristic-based optimization approaches struggle to keep pace with the increasing diversity of architectures and programming models. AI4HPCC aims to address these challenges by fostering discussion on AI-enhanced techniques that can automate, generalize, and accelerate the compiler optimization pipeline ‚Äî from code analysis and transformation to performance modeling and autotuning.

Call for Papers

We invite speakers from academia and industry to present their work on the following topics (including but not limited to):

  • Machine learning-based compiler optimization and code generation
  • AI-driven performance modeling, prediction, and optimization
  • Large language models for compiler development
  • Integration of AI-enhanced compilers with HPC runtimes and programming models
  • Benchmarks, datasets, and tooling for AI-based compiler research

The workshop will publish its proceedings with the ICS 2026 conference. Authors must follow the same formatting guidelines as main conference papers (ACM template, \documentclass[sigconf]{acmart} in LaTeX). Submitted manuscripts may not exceed eight (8) pages in length for regular papers and at least (4) pages for short papers, excluding references.
